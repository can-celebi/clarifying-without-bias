\title{\vspace{-2.5em}Stress-Testing Instruction Chatbots for Economics Experiments:\\
\large Which Workflow and Model Best Preserve Treatment Integrity?}
\author{Can \c{C}elebi}
\date{\small Preliminary --- \today}

\begin{document}
\maketitle
\vspace{-1.8em}

\noindent
An instruction chatbot deployed inside an economics experiment is not a help tool---it is part of
the \emph{manipulation}. It must clarify rules and refuse to supply advice, expected-value
computations, or strategic hints. We built an adversarial \textbf{stress-test harness} that pits
synthetic ``subject'' personas against the bot over thousands of multi-turn conversations and scores
every reply with an independent LLM judge on three metrics: bias-leak (the integrity metric), factual
error, and incoherence. We then asked a single deployment question: \emph{which pipeline architecture
and which model leak the least?}

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Design.} A two-phase plan over two scenarios (an easier mid-experiment context and a
$\sim$2$\times$ harder cold-start context) and seven adversarial personas, 15 runs $\times$ 15 turns
per cell. \textbf{Phase~A} (15,750 judged turns) screens five architectures, from a single
all-in-one call to a classifier $\to$ router $\to$ per-class corrector, all at the cheapest model.
\textbf{Phase~B} escalates the winner one lever at a time: a corrector pass, reasoning effort
(low/medium/high), and model capability.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Headline results.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Simpler is safer.} The single-call pipeline is Pareto-dominant on bias-leak, cost, and
    latency in every cell. Every added agent (classifier, low-confidence probe gate, corrector)
    leaves bias the same or worse---a $1.2$--$4.1\times$ penalty---because the extra steps reframe and
    re-surface the subject's question, which is itself a contamination channel.
  \item \textbf{Capability is the only lever that works.} Holding the winning architecture fixed,
    upgrading the base model (\texttt{nano}$\to$\texttt{mini}) cut bias-leak \textbf{46\%} on the
    easier scenario and \textbf{68\%} on the harder one, improved factuality, and ran faster at
    non-binding cost. Reasoning effort was neutral-to-harmful at every level tested; a corrector pass
    made bias \emph{worse}.
  \item \textbf{Integrity is a per-conversation property.} Single-turn leak risk is small but
    compounds across a 15-turn dialogue; we report per-conversation consistency, not single-shot
    accuracy.
\end{itemize}

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Caveat.} The headline upgrade cell currently shares one model between bot and judge,
so a small self-leniency artefact cannot yet be excluded; an independent-judge confirmation cell is in
flight. We therefore state the finding as \emph{``model capability is the lever that works,''} not as
a final production recommendation.
